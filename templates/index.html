About Us

LogiChain is built upon the XRP blockchain network, we use XRP to facilitate quick, transparent, and effective payments between supply chain networks. LogiChain uses XRP’s blockchain to store data about the movement of products through the supply chain, meaning a product can be traced back to its source in a matter of seconds

This transparency of product production means that customers are naturally inclined to trust the product more than others, simply because finding the source of the product can be as simple as scanning a QR code; this way there is no questioning the product’s authenticity. On the topic of trust, LogiChain promotes the use of integrity verification through the supply chain so that a customer knows that whatever it is they’re purchasing, it's safe for them to use.

But, Why Us?

You've heard about some features of LogiChain, but lets drill down into some more detailed points about why you should choose LogiChain to manage your logistics.

  • 01 Blockchain-Powered Efficiency

    Since a blockchain is always available to all members of the supply chain, time will never be wasted querying companies for past data who would then need to dig through records to find information.

    Blockchain allows for smart contracts which allows automation of payments and notifies members of the members of the supply chain.

    Since all data stored on the blockchain is in a universal format so users of LogiChain can always easily digest necessary information.

  • 02 Data Safety

    Data stored on the blockchain is safe from data loss; a blockchain is, in essence, a distributed ledger. So if one member of the supply chain deletes all of its data, all information will continue to be safe with other members.

    Any data put onto the blockchain cannot be modified, so if someone were to try modify XRP’s blockchain data, they would need more computing power than the entire XRP network, which is not feasible.

  • 03 Variable Transparency

    Since LogiChain can operate on private XRP networks containing only the supply chain’s network of members, the movements of the supply chain don't have to be open to the public. However, customers can always track single products through the supply chain regardless of whether or not all operations are public.
